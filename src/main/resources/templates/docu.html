Select Sequences For Visualization

View the virus genome, proteome and other information

Select a species and click "Select" button to view: Genome Browser - Select Species.

Perform sequence alignment and view the results

Select the sequence to be aligned: Multiple Alignment - Select Sequences. First, select a species in the "All Sequences" table. You can see the whole genome, genes and proteins of the species. Click "Return" to return to the species list.

The sequence you selected will appear in the "Select List". Click the "Remove From List" button to remove the sequence. Click the "Submit" button to submit the sequences for alignment, and we will return your job ID and a view link. After the comparison, click the link to view the comparison results.

Note: you can only select two or more homologous sequences for alignment, such as gene protein alignment is not allowed. For performance reasons, we recommend that you choose no more than 4 Virus Genome sequences, or no more than 20 gene sequences, or no more than 50 protein sequences.

Select SARS-CoV-2 strains to view sequence evolution

There are nearly 4000 aligned representative SARS-CoV-2 virus strains for you to choose from: SARS-CoV-2 Evolution - Select Strains. You can screen strains by searching the name, collection time or selecting the evolutionary strain, location and other conditions. Click on the line to view the details of the sample. Note that your selection will not be cancelled due to page turning or changing the filter conditions. Click "Submit" button to visualize the selected strains.

Select Visual Range In Genome Browser

To left button

If there is something on the left side of the visual range, you can click this button to move to the left.

Note: All the buttons in the toolbar will automatically check for clickability. For example, if it is already on the far left, this button turns gray and cannot be clicked.

To right button

Similar to "To left" button, but in the opposite direction.

Zoom out button

If the current visual range is not the maximum, clicking this button will enlarge your field of vision up to 3 times.

Zoom in button

Similar to "Zoom out" button, but to reduce the visual range.

Go backward button

Undo the range selection you just made.

Go forward button

Redo the range selection you just made.

Positioning tool

The positioning tool is located in the toolbar and displays the start, end and length of the current visual range. You can enter the start stop position and click the "Go" button to jump.

Note: for visual effect, please select a region with at least 25 bases.

Overview track

Overview track represents the whole sequence. The orange box represents the position of the current visible range. You can select an area on the track or drag the orange box to jump to that area.

Scale track

Scale track represents the current visible area. Similarly, you can select some areas on the track to see the details.

Drag movable track

"Movable track" here refers to all tracks except overview track and scale track. As you drag, all the elements on these tracks will follow.

Display Of Multiple Sequence Alignment Results

Aligned sequences

In the genome browser, all the sequences are aligned, and the missing bases/amino acids are filled with "-" relative to other sequences. If you want to know the original position of a base/amino acid in an aligned sequence, please pay attention to the note on the right side of the sequence, which indicates the position of the leftmost and rightmost base/amino acid in the original sequence.

Sequence similarity

There are three tracks to show the sequence similarity of a site from different aspects: consensus, which only focuses on those bases/amino acids that are the same in all sequences; Conservation, focusing on the proportion of major bases/amino acids; Sequence diversity, which uses Shannon entropy to measure the degree of variation of a sequence.

Phylogenetic tree

The phylogenetic tree was constructed by neighbor joining method, and the evolutionary relationship among these species was inferred according to the sequence similarity. The closer the evolution distance, the closer the two species are in the evolutionary tree.

Display Of SARS-CoV-2 Sequence Evolution

Mode switching

After selecting several virus strains and visualizing them, they enter the pack mode by default. In pack mode, all the strains were assigned to different clades and displayed according to the clades. Click the toggle switch in the toolbar to switch to full mode. In full mode, all the virus sequences will be displayed in the genome browser, and the specific information of all strains will also be displayed in the table below.

Mutation display

At a smaller scale, the indicator bar and color in the sequence track represent the mutation and the base after mutation compared with the reference sequence. At the base scale, bases different from the reference sequence will be highlighted. For insertion mutation, click the insertion site to view the inserted sequence.

Sequence diversity track

Shannon entropy of each base site in the current selected sequence was calculated and displayed in the form of histogram.

Note: ignore insertion mutation and base "N".

Mutation frequency track

The proportion of each base site in the current selected sequence different from the reference sequence was calculated and displayed in the form of dot graph.

Note: considering the inconsistent sequencing quality, only the mutation caused by base substitution was calculated.

Phylogenetic Tree

Display the position of the virus in the evolutionary tree. Click on the strains node to view the details.

Let the sequence evolve

In pack mode, it shows the variation of the latest virus in each branch of evolution on a certain day. The date is displayed in the toolbar, and on the right side of it is a time control. You can drag or click the time bar, or click the start/pause/continue button or stop button to see the evolution of these sequences over time.

Sequence diversity, mutation frequency and phylogenetic tree will be updated according to the currently displayed sequence. The virus information form will also be updated.
